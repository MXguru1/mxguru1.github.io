## Executive Summary

We ran 103 adversarial wargame rounds pitting cloud-scale attacker models (397B–671B params) against a local 8-agent swarm on a single RTX 5070 (12GB VRAM). Defense rate climbed from 53% to 82% across three iterations through model swaps, a novel "Defender Vanguard" prompt injection technique, and live telemetry-driven hardening. Authority escalation detection jumped from 44% to 90% after Vanguard injection. Social engineering remains the weakest vector at 50%.

## Methodology

**Hardware:** NVIDIA RTX 5070, 12GB VRAM, single GPU. Zero cloud dependency.

**Attacker models (cloud-routed via Ollama):**
- DeepSeek-V3.2 (671B)
- Qwen 3.5 (397B)
- Gemma 4 (31B)

**Defender models:**
- Auditor: DeepSeek-Coder-V2 16B (88% → 96% detect rate)
- Trace Forensics: Qwen2.5-Coder 1.5B (90% detect rate)
- Guardian: nexus-tiny 1.2B → Vanguard-injected (56% → 76%)
- Sentinel: nexus-tiny 1.2B → Vanguard-injected (60% → 66%)
- Supply Chain: nexus-tiny 1.2B → Vanguard-injected (66% → 72%)

**Attack categories:** authority_escalation, prompt_injection, social_engineering, logic_bomb, context_poisoning.

**Consensus mechanism:** 5 defenders vote independently. Threshold: ≥3/5 = defended.

## Findings

### Run 1: Baseline (100 rounds, code model defender)
Overall defense rate: **53%** (53/100)

| Category | Defended | Breached | Rate |
|----------|----------|----------|------|
| social_engineering | 12 | 7 | 63% |
| context_poisoning | 12 | 7 | 63% |
| logic_bomb | 12 | 10 | 55% |
| prompt_injection | 8 | 10 | 44% |
| authority_escalation | 9 | 13 | **41%** |

**Analysis:** DeepSeek-Coder-V2 is optimized for code, not adversarial intent detection. The three nexus-tiny 1.2B defenders consistently abstained on authority_escalation, dragging consensus to 2/5 even when the auditor caught attacks.

### Run 2: Soldier Upgrade (50 rounds, DeepSeek auditor)
Overall defense rate: **78%** (39/50)

Auditor detection rate improved from 62% to 88%. DeepSeek-V3.2 breach rate halved (78% → 45%). But the structural 2/5 consensus problem persisted — the tiny models still couldn't detect authority patterns.

### Run 3: Vanguard Injection (50 rounds, red-team-aware defenders)
Overall defense rate: **82%** (41/50)

| Category | v6.1 Rate | v6.2 (Vanguard) | Delta |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| authority_escalation | 44% | **90%** | +46pp |
| context_poisoning | 77% | 88% | +11pp |
| logic_bomb | 86% | **100%** | +14pp |
| prompt_injection | 85% | 75% | -10pp |
| social_engineering | 100% | **50%** | -50pp |

**The Vanguard technique:** We injected a "Defender Vanguard" system prompt into the three 1.2B models — teaching them to think like red teamers when evaluating inputs. Same model weights, same VRAM footprint, different mindset. The guardian's detection rate jumped from 56% to 76%.

**The tradeoff:** Vanguard training was authority/escalation-heavy. Social engineering detection collapsed from 100% to 50% — the models shifted attention. Defense was displaced, not fully solved.

## Improvements Applied

1. **Model swap:** Replaced non-existent `llama-tulu3-8b` with DeepSeek-Coder-V2 16B as auditor
2. **Vanguard injection:** Red-team-aware system prompts for 3 tiny defenders (zero VRAM cost)
3. **Live telemetry:** Grafana dashboard with per-category defense rates updated every 5 seconds
4. **Auto-healer (deploying):** Blocklist patching + prompt antibodies from breach data
5. **Threat vaccines (deploying):** arXiv + MITRE ATLAS harvesting for proactive hardening

## Next Steps

1. **Fix social_engineering regression** — balance Vanguard prompt to cover social manipulation alongside technical attacks
2. **Lower consensus threshold** to 2/5 for categories where auditor + trace_forensics agree (would have caught 8/11 breaches)
3. **Deploy auto-healer** — every breach creates a vaccine for the remaining rounds
4. **Scale to 500 rounds** — validate that defense rate holds under sustained pressure
5. **Publish methodology** — open-source the wargame framework for community red-teaming
